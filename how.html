Reduce Fuel Consumption

Consider taking public transport more often, or look into purchasing an electric or hybrid car as your next vehicle. Carpooling can save you money, reduce greenhouse gas emissions and traffic congestion.

Reduce • Reuse • Recycle

Consider buying more secondhand stuff. Bring your own bags when shopping, and avoid products with excessive packaging and plastic. Purchase reusable products instead of one-time-use things.

Change to LED Lightbulbs

The old traditional bulbs gave up 90% of their energy off as heat. LEDS (if properly certified) use 1 quarter of the energy of old traditional bulbs, and last 25 times longer.

Plant a Tree

Trees help remove carbon dioxide from the atmosphere, one at about 10 years of age, are estimated to soak up about 48 pounds of CO2 per year.

Solar Panels

Add solar panels to the roof of your home. This costs a little more, but many providers offer financing options which minimize upfront costs.

Avoid Flying

Although aviation is a relatively small industry, it has a disproportionately large impact on the climate system. So consider taking vacations closer to home, find another means of transportation. If you must fly, minimize number of flights and take the most direct route.
